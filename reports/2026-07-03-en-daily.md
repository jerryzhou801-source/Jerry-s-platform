> **Rapeseed/RSO/Biodiesel:** RSO fob Dutch mill eased across the curve (ASO €1,142.50, −€4) on weaker Euronext, energy and FX; funds hold a six-year-high seasonal net long but have started trimming. Bulgaria's first cuts show 44–45pc oil (crop possibly 500kt vs 300kt LY), yet Bulgarian, Romanian and German farmers are all withholding new crop; ASO crush margins slipped €5 to \~€68/t while the spot RSO–RME spread collapsed to €26.50/t from €63/t. Norway's 50pc tariff on Russian/Belarusian RSO shifts \~230,000 t/yr of non-GMO demand toward EU/Ukraine — soft short-term tone, medium-term supported.
>
> **Canola:** ICE Winnipeg Nov fell >1pc on StatCan's record 2026-27 Canadian plantings; Australian new-crop WA fob was cut $15/wk to $585/t on farmer selling, with Prairie waterlogging and a possible China–Australia reopening the swing factors. Canadian old-crop offers under $600/t cfr China still face the 15pc duty.
>
> **Vegoils/SBO/macro:** New-crop SFO holds >$100/t below old crop (OND $1,372.50 vs JAS $1,482.50) on a projected sunseed rebound to 62mn t. CBOT Aug SBO fell 2.3pc w/w to 66.69¢/lb with WTI; US crush margins broke below $3/bu. China and the US agreed "in principle" to cut ag tariffs — the 10pc soybean duty is the watch item, with only 200kt booked against China's 25mn t 2026-27 obligation.
