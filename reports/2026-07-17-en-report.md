Rapeseed/RSO/Crush margins: The week's defining move is in the margin, not the flat price: the indicative ASO crush margin — 40pc oil, 55pc meal, ex-transport — rose every day this week to €106/t on 16 July, up €35 from 10 July and above €100 for the first time since early May, with spot margins around €138/t. The decomposition matters: RSO spot and ASO surged on Rhine and Ukraine supply expectations, RSM ASO jumped €23 to €253/t (delivery problems, strong demand, plus Bunge's Mannheim outage), while Euronext August futures gained only €19.25 — hitting a €541 high Wednesday before easing to €535.75 Thursday as new-crop harvest pressure capped seed. Physical seed still trades €11 over the August board for ASO. Friday's curve rotated rather than extended: spot -€8.50 to €1,325 as sellers lowered offers, ASO +€17.50 to €1,275, deferreds +€4.50-5.50 — sellers are cautious on July-August delivery risk (some withdrawn from spot entirely) even as Kaub, at just 40-49cm above reference on 14-16 July with barges at half loads or less, is forecast to recover to 85cm on 20 July, above the 75cm operational limit. In Ukraine, only a few traders bid cpt POC and lowered again to protect export margins — rapeseed cpt POC -$11 to $577.50 — with harvest starting in the southwest on better-than-expected yields but oil content of 40-43pc versus ~45pc last year. Net tone: the crush-margin blowout is the position of record; if the Rhine normalises from Monday and offers keep retreating, the spot RSO premium and margin spike are both exposed to mean reversion, while Ukrainian export capacity remains the structural bull leg.

SFO/Black Sea: The relative-value marker of the week — spot SFO fob six ports closed Thursday at a $50/t discount to spot RSO (from a $5 discount Wednesday), against a typically sizeable premium: RSO drew energy-complex and biofuel strength while SFO, a food-sector oil, lagged. Some prompt trade returned (August-loading at $1,475 Thursday), but participants doubt the discount sparks much demand — old-crop SFO needs are mostly covered, EU heatwaves and adverse Ukrainian weather cloud new-crop supply, and EU food consumers may shift toward RSO in October-September on SFO availability concerns. Supply-side, POC activity has collapsed to committed voyages only, remaining shipowners are pricing higher war-risk freight into sellers' margins, and Russia's Azov paralysis reads through directly: with Kerch closed and Azov ports severely limited, crushers unable to move accumulated stocks may cut sunseed purchases from farmers off the late-August harvest — a mechanism to cinch Russian SFO supply into the new season. The wheat market quantified the logistics divergence: CVB commands a $25/t premium over Novorossiysk and $30 over POC, versus norms of $2-4.50. Net tone: the RSO-over-SFO inversion is a fundamentals split (energy/biofuel vs food demand) sitting on top of a shared Black Sea supply shock; both legs of Black Sea SFO (Ukraine and Russia) are tightening simultaneously into new crop.

EU new-crop/Global canola/China: Argus published its EU rapeseed forecasts: France 4.82mn t base case (4.58 low) versus 4.64mn t last year — heat impact less severe than feared, with losses tracing to April cold snaps at flowering, oil content as high as 46pc, and larger area offsetting a yield decline to 3.4 t/ha from 3.66; Germany 4.15mn t base (3.95 low) versus 4mn t, though early southern results disappoint; Poland 3.38mn t versus 3.5mn t; Romania up at 2.88mn t from 2.5mn t and Bulgaria \~390,000-400,000t (+100,000). Five Romanian cargoes are due into NWE ports in July. In Australia, Giwa held WA canola near-flat at 4.39mn t within a winter crop down 17pc — conditions favourable now, but El Niño plus a positive Indian Ocean Dipole from September threaten the spring finish; canola fob WA jumped $37.50 to $626 (Sep). Canada's old-crop exports narrowed the gap to 7pc below last year at 8.56mn t, above the five-year average. In China, RSM inventories (\~210,000t) are set to build from August-September as crush rates rise above 30pc, aquaculture demand seasonally fades and new-crop Canadian/Australian canola lands after November. Indonesia's B50 formally took effect 1 July but awaits producer allocations — 16.7-18mn kL implies 15.2-16.3mn t CPO feedstock, with capacity adequacy in question. Net tone: EU new-crop is adequate-not-ample with Romania the upside surprise; medium-term, Australian weather and B50 execution are the swing factors, while China's meal side softens into Q4.
