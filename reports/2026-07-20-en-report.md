Rapeseed/RSO/Logistics: The character of the move changed: futures led and the deferred curve caught up. Euronext August rapeseed rose €13.25 to €557.25, and fob Dutch mill repriced as prompt +€15 to €1,340, NDJ +€14.50 to €1,203.50, FMA +€13.50 to €1,191.50 — with ASO the pause point at -€2.50 to €1,272.50 after last week's surge. That deferred strength arrives despite Rhine relief: Kaub recovered to 89cm on 20 July from the ultra-critical 40cm of 15 July (long-term average 208cm), though freight respite may be short-lived as levels are seen falling again in late July. The market is instead pricing Argus' structural revisions from the weekly wrap: heat-linked new-crop losses of 600,000t across France, Germany and Poland, and an EU 2026-27 import requirement revised up 400,000t to 5.5mn t — squarely into a blockaded Ukraine, GMO-constrained and crush-hungry Canada, and an El Niño-threatened Australia. Ukraine's harvest is 16pc threshed (20-30pc in central/southern regions, ~430,000t cut), and the inland-versus-border split widened: rapeseed cpt POC -$10 to $567.50 and cpt Reni/Izmail -$19.50, while dap Constanta rose $13.50 to $624.50 — port purchases cut, cargoes redirected west, farmers selling rapeseed first for cash flow. Net tone: the bull case has migrated from spot logistics to the structural balance (import gap, heat losses, export-route risk); short-term, Rhine normalisation removes one prop just as the board adds another.

Ukraine bottleneck/SFO: Argus quantified the coming storage crunch: with 2026-27 output projected at 83.65mn t on record yields and POC near-totally blockaded, Ukraine faces a >35mn t storage deficit once corn arrives in October — silos already 21pc full on 1 July (11mn t, including 5.4mn t old-crop corn). Alternative routes offer \~4mn t/month (land 1.6-1.8mn, Danube peak 2.4mn) against peak-season needs, a \~1.5mn t/month deficit, and cost double: $50-55/t road-to-river versus $25-30 rail-to-Odesa. The read-through for the vegoil complex: wheat exports slow first (deepwater-dependent, EU quota just 1.3mn t), and scarce land capacity gets rationed toward high-unit-value rapeseed and SFO — a durable reshaping of Ukrainian flows. SFO priced it: NWE six-ports OND jumped $62.50 to $1,470 and JFM +$42.50 to $1,427.50 against spot +$30 to $1,497.50 — the deferred legs again outrunning spot as Argus cut EU 2026-27 sunseed production to 9.3mn t (from 9.85mn t) and pegged Ukraine's at 10.9mn t, the lowest in over a decade, with the Russian-harvest crusher-outlet risk still live into late August. Weekend strikes continued on both sides (183 'shadow fleet' vessels hit since 6 July; Russian missiles striking vessels and facilities at POC). Net tone: the new-crop SFO balance is deteriorating on both supply and logistics simultaneously; the deferred SFO curve is where that structural tightening is being expressed.

Global oilseeds/China/US: China's board crush margins collapsed to -251 yuan/t on 17 July from -127 a week earlier as Brazilian offers chased rising US Gulf values (two October USG cargoes traded at +304¢/bu), and buying slowed to around eight Brazilian cargoes — mostly March 2027 at ~95¢/bu, where exporters accepted flat levels against a 15¢ deal-offer spread to pre-sell a record 2026-27 crop; participants see slow buying until margins approach breakeven, likely not before mid-August. CBOT soybeans hit a two-month high (+23.25¢ intraday moves; November settled at 1,226.25¢) on steady Chinese US-crop demand and geopolitics, pulling Brazilian port premiums sharply lower (Feb -7.50 to +20¢). Demand-side, China's H1 industrial feed output hit a record 168.5mn t (+3.9pc), led by record pig feed (+7.9pc), though corn's ration share fell to 33.8pc and SBM's to 13.2pc on cheap wheat substitution. India's June vegoil imports fell 29pc y/y to 1.15mn t (SFO 242,870t, from 295,726 in May), with SEAI attributing firm global prices to biofuel-mandate feedstock diversion. US crops are a bearish counterweight: corn 67pc and soybeans 66pc good-to-excellent, both ahead of average pace into pollination with a benign week forecast. Net tone: import-economics friction (China margins) and benign US weather cap the soy complex short-term, while structural biofuel demand and record Chinese feed output support the medium-term floor.
