Rapeseed/RSO/Canada: The correction flagged on Friday materialised, with the front leading it down: prompt -€25 to €1,315, ASO -€30 to €1,242.50, against NDJ -€4 and FMA -€5.50 — traders covered shorts further forward while near-curve buying interest disappeared, so the spot premium built on Rhine/Ukraine friction deflated fastest. The Canada axis turned genuinely two-sided overnight. Bearish: Trump announced sweeping 50pc tariffs on Canadian agricultural products among other goods, effective from a 19 August deadline and applying regardless of USMCA — if realised and applied to canola, displaced Canadian exports would seek alternative outlets, with the EU the natural destination; ICE canola showed little reaction so far, and Carney says a comprehensive deal remains the goal. Structurally bearish too: AAFC raised its 2026-27 canola crop forecast by 1.8mn t to 21mn t on record seeded area of ~9.5mn ha (a 6pc swing out of wheat after China eased tariffs on Canadian seed), though it routed the increase domestically — record 13.5mn t crush (+500,000t, on capacity build-out and US biofuel-driven oil demand) with exports still cut to 8mn t. Prairie conditions improved (Saskatchewan 76pc good-to-excellent, topsoil moisture 87pc adequate). Net tone: short-term corrective as the logistics premium unwinds and Canada supply news lands; the tariff is the new binary — a 50pc wall pushing 21mn t of crop toward non-US outlets would be a material bearish overhang for the EU import price, but it is threatened, not enacted.

Ukraine inland collapse/SFO: The bottleneck has moved from freight rates into farmer income: Ukrainian domestic prices fell hard again — rapeseed cpt POC -$25 to $542.50, cpt Reni/Izmail -$17.50 to $550, sunseed cpt POC -$27.50 to $610, wheat cpt POC -$10.50-11.50, feed barley cpt -$12.50 — as farmers sell "widely to meet urgent cash needs," some offloading newly-harvested winter crops at or near production cost, switching crop-to-crop for any marginal profit. Every alternative route is degrading at once: trucks scarce and road freight climbing, Polish/central European rail maintenance until late August, Danube water levels falling with river-port bids sharply cut, Constanta elevators full. Meanwhile dap Constanta rapeseed rose again (+$2 to €548) — the inland-to-border spread keeps blowing out, transferring the entire logistics cost onto Ukrainian producers. In SFO, spot NWE retraced -$27.50 to $1,470 and OND -$12.50, but the trade record shows the forward bid is real and rising: OND traded $1,460 on 20 July versus $1,410 on 16 July, JFM at $1,385, as European buyers book new-crop rather than wait out an open-ended disruption; cif Mersin jumped $28.50 to $1,453.50 (Russian-origin cargo at $1,446), with Turkish buyers expected to chase spot. Argentina is the release valve: June SFO exports more than doubled to a record 251,000t on record crush. Net tone: inland Ukraine is in a buyers' market bordering on distress while destination and forward markets stay bid — the spread structure, not flat price, is carrying the war premium; watch whether farmer distress selling at the border eventually caps EU-delivered values.

Global/China/EU corn: China's DCE SBM rose to 3,138 yuan/t (from 3,069 on 17 July) on CBOT strength — attributed to adverse US weather — plus a domestic supply wrinkle: damage to Zhoushan port from a 20 July accident, with some participants seeing two-to-three-month repairs disrupting soybean clearance and lifting eastern spot basis (to -200 yuan from -210), though others expect record June arrivals to absorb it. CBOT itself eased (November -3.50 to 1,222.75¢) as US crop conditions beat expectations into pollination, and Brazilian premiums kept sliding (Feb -2.50 to +17.50¢; March 2027 dealt at 18-20¢ discounts) with China withdrawing counter-seasonally. In EU feed, Argus formalised the corn cut: EU 48.1mn t (-3.9mn from the prior estimate, -9.8mn y/y), France 6.9mn t versus 9.3mn at the start of the month and 13.2mn last year — yields below 7 t/ha for the first time this century, 175,000 ha diverted to silage, French exports collapsing to 2.2mn t from 6.6mn — with Euronext November above €250 and a near-record ~$100 premium over CBOT December; the read-through for oilseeds is feed-sector substitution toward wheat and oilseed meals rather than import pull (EU imports seen at just 19mn t). Net tone: the soy complex is capped short-term by benign US weather and Chinese margin weakness, but the EU feed-energy deficit quietly adds a demand leg under European meals into Q4.
