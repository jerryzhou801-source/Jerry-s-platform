Rapeseed/RSO/Biodiesel: RSO fell (prompt -€3 to €1,231/t, ASO €1,149) as firmer meal supported crush margins — the meal feedback capping oil; Paris futures rose less sharply as French yield fears eased. Ukrainian seed prices kept climbing (cpt POC $580/t) with harvest thin until H2 July, while biodiesel demand stays weak: the RME–RSO spread narrowed to ~€67/t from €83/t, squeezing esterification margins. Short-term consolidative; early-harvest tightness keeps seed basis firm.

China/Global flows: CBOT soybeans surged \~50¢/bu on reported Chinese purchases of US beans (state-owned buyer), with the market expecting China's 10% tariff to be removed — opening commercial crushers to the 25mn t 2026-27 commitment vs last year's 12mn t SOE-only. Structurally, China's RSO imports look capped near 2mn t/yr: global exportable supply is only \~8mn t, Canada's ~4mn t exports flow almost entirely to US renewable diesel, and Europe competes with China for Australian canola. Tariff removal is a short-term bullish catalyst for the whole complex.

Sunflower/SFO: SFO spot held firm ($1,475) on thin old-crop supply while forward quarters sank (OND $1,350), pushing the spot/front-quarter spread above $100/t — the widest since 2022 — as record USDA global SFS output prices in the Black Sea rebound despite French heat risk. Old-crop tight, new-crop heavy.
