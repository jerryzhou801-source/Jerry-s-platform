Rapeseed/RSO/Logistics: The deferred legs capitulated — after Tuesday's prompt-only kink, the whole fob Dutch mill curve repriced: prompt +€43.50 to €1,289.50, ASO +€34 to €1,225, NDJ +€13.50 to €1,187, FMA +€10 to €1,172.50. The driver is a full supply-side stack: Euronext rapeseed futures shot up as much as €15/t front-month, and fob Dutch mill RSO offers rose about €50/t at the front of the curve, though buyers were unwilling to follow, slowing activity — so today's marks are offer-led, not trade-validated. The structural case is stark: a maritime blockade of one of the EU's biggest rapeseed suppliers could tighten European supply quickly — Ukraine provided 37pc of the bloc's imports over the past five marketing years, and the EU's crush capacity exceeds its own rapeseed output. Land routes are mainly viable for high-value oilseeds and processed products, where unit value better offsets transport costs, but trucks and rail cannot fully replace deep-sea capacity and cost more, while Romanian alternative ports are at peak season with domestic crops and have little to no spare capacity. On top: Rhine low water keeps restricting barges, with crushers along the river facing higher freight on seed receipts and RSO/RSM deliveries, and the fob Moselle market seeing limited activity even as harvest progresses. Energy adds a third leg — gasoil futures rallied this week on Middle East tensions keeping Hormuz flows limited. Ukrainian seed itself was steady-to-firmer (cpt POC unchanged at $595, dap Constanta +$7 to $606). Net tone: broad-based bullish repricing on supply friction from three independent sources (Black Sea, Rhine, energy); the offer-bid gap says the flat price hasn't cleared yet — watch whether buyers pay up or the offer-led move retraces.

Black Sea shutdown/SFO: Ukraine's deepwater grain exports ground to a halt after vessels were hit on 14-15 July; traders suspended POC operations at the peak of the winter harvest, and shipowners are withdrawing tonnage from the corridor. The capacity math is unforgiving: deep-water ports peaked at 5.4mn t/month versus 1.8mn t for land routes and 2.4mn t via the Danube — which itself is now risky after attacks on river infrastructure. Wheat quantified the shock: Euronext Sep +€15 to €231.50 (from €205 on 9 July, pre-Kerch), CVB 12.5pc +$23.50 to $270.50, Russia Novorossiysk +$7.50 to $241.50 — while Ukrainian cpt POC wheat fell $6.50-7.50, so the fob–cpt wedge blew out further: the war premium is being paid at the water and extracted from inland sellers simultaneously. In SFO the premium formally reached NWE fob: OND +$36.75 to $1,397.50 and JFM +$35 to $1,362.50 against spot +$7.50 to $1,470 — the deferred jump prices Ukrainian new-crop export risk, exactly the migration flagged yesterday. Destination kept climbing too (cif Mersin +$20 to $1,425). Net tone: the risk premium has gone from a cif/destination phenomenon to a structural NWE fob one in a day; unless deepwater security is restored, Ukraine's 2026-27 export campaign faces a severe bottleneck during peak supply.

Global vegoils/China: Chinese private buyers booked two Australian new-crop canola cargoes for Q4 — one October–November and one November–December — the first trades since GAC approved Australian canola imports on Monday. That validates Monday's approval as real demand rather than symbolic, though Q4 timing confirms no near-term relief for tight Chinese supply. In inter-oil spreads, the DCE SBO–palm olein spread narrowed to -686 yuan/t, its tightest since late January (from -1,016 on 23 June), on rising DCE SBO tracking Chicago while palm weakened on larger Malaysian June stocks; palm olein cfr south China nonetheless rose $23 to $1,168. In soybeans, China's September coverage exceeds 65pc — ahead of typical pace — but October–January remains thin (28pc October, 5pc November, December-January largely uncovered) on US import policy uncertainty, with July arrivals seen below 12mn t and August below 8mn t after June's record. Supply-side, Conab nudged Brazil's 2025-26 crop to a record 360.1mn t, soybeans to a record 180.6mn t (+5.3pc y/y), crush to 62.6mn t and SBO output to 12.6mn t — and Brazilian offers to China keep rising, likely through September as exporters converge on US Gulf values, worsening board crush margins. Net tone: record South American supply and softer palm cap the complex medium-term, but China's rapeseed tightness persists until Australian arrivals land late-year.
