Rapeseed/RSO/RED III: The front-quarter reclaimed leadership: ASO +€40.50 to €1,283 with spot unchanged at €1,315 after Tuesday's heavy buying, NDJ +€14 to €1,213.50 and FMA +€10.50 — the ASO-prompt spread compressed to €32 from €72.50, a steepening that says the market is rolling its tightness expectation from July-August logistics into the new-crop quarter. Two structural inputs justify it. First, demand policy: Spain adopted RED III, with the crop-based cap expected to rise to 7pc from 2.6pc — a direct, mandate-driven RSO demand add in a major EU market, with wider implementation expected to support biodiesel demand. Second, the crush ambition: some participants now see EU 2026-27 rapeseed crush above 28mn t, requiring more imports from Canada and Australia — and if POC disruption runs past September, RSO is positioned to take a greater share of EU food-sector consumption at SFO's expense (French sunseed dryness reinforces this). Supply-side, Germany's first new-crop deliveries arrive within days, with below-average early yields expected to improve as harvest broadens. In Ukraine, the inland rout deepened: rapeseed cpt POC -$22.50 to $520 (cumulative -$57.50 since Friday), cpt Reni/Izmail -$12.50 to $537.50, and notably dap Izov -$37.50 — the western land border is now also congesting — while dap Constanta held at €547. Net tone: structurally firmer on the demand stack (RED III, crush build, food-sector substitution); the compressed prompt-ASO spread is the market voting that tightness persists into Q4 rather than resolving with the Rhine.

Black Sea/Export triage/Wheat: Argus put numbers on the capacity rationing: since 6 July at dap Constanta, rapeseed +€41.5/t versus milling wheat +€26.5, corn +€19, feed barley +€18 — the queue at the last functioning deep-water hub is explicitly repricing toward unit value, confirming Monday's thesis that scarce logistics get rationed toward oilseeds. Ukraine's exports did tick up (2.09mn t by 22 July from 1.71mn on the 17th) — pre-escalation bookings still loading — but fresh strikes on Odesa infrastructure hit the afternoon, and the legal deadlock is tightening: ports officially remain open, so force majeure can't be declared on existing contracts, while buyers demand delivery guarantees sellers can't provide. Inland wheat cpt POC fell another $8.50-9.50 to $174-175 as farmers sell straight from the field for cash. Russia is converging on the same squeeze: Novorossiysk closed to vessel movements 00:00-05:00 daily until at least 27 July, silos filling with new crop, and Argus cut Russian 2026-27 exports to 45mn t (from 47mn) and production to 89.7mn t (from 91.2mn), with July shipments at a potential decade-low 1.5mn t; a 90pc freight-subsidy scheme for Rostov-to-Novorossiysk rerouting drew a mixed response. Global wheat repriced: Paris front-month +4pc late-session, HRW +$11.21, Rouen +€9.50, Argentina +$10 — with Russia and Ukraine set to supply 29pc of world wheat exports, the deadlock is now a global-price event. SFO consolidated (NWE spot -$15 to $1,455, OND -$17.50 to $1,440, still ~$43 over five days; cpt POC -$25). Net tone: the war premium is institutionalising — into port queue priorities, legal structures, and sovereign subsidy schemes — which makes it stickier than a freight spike; wheat is now exporting the Black Sea risk globally.

China/Global vegoils: China's demand pulse stayed soft but the supply response is starting: Brazilian exporters cut offers despite weaker CBOT — storage pressure after China took fewer than 10 cargoes last week versus a 15/week average last month — which could nurse board crush margins back toward workable; SBM stocks keep building with crush above 60pc and heading to \~70pc next week, capping DCE upside. Palm demand stayed muted a second week: near-curve import margins \~-200 yuan/t, August needs already >60pc covered (\~130,000t booked), DCE PO easing on peak-season output and SBO stocks up 6pc w/w to \~1.4mn t — China's whole oils complex is amply supplied into Q3. The rapeseed-relevant flow: China's H1 RSM imports hit a record 1.62mn t (+4.5pc), June +39pc y/y with Canada's share jumping to 41pc from 16pc post-tariff-removal (Canadian RSM at $313/t vs India $273, Russia $328); participants expect flows to normalise toward Canada as primary supplier, India redirecting to southeast Asia — Canada's re-established meal channel into China sits alongside the AAFC record-crush plan as another reason Canadian product bypasses the EU. US biofuel demand stayed constructive: SBO fob Iowa +1.18¢ to 78.98¢, ethanol output +5.2pc. Net tone: China caps the global oils complex short-term on ample stocks and negative margins, while the EU's mandate-and-crush stack pulls the other way — the divergence widens.
