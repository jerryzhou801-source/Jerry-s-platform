Rapeseed/RSO: Forward RSO re-rated sharply (ASO +€18.50 to €1,167.50/t, NDJ +€20 to €1,168) on crush economics — Romanian harvest shows high oil content and above-average yields, and the implied rapeseed discount averaged €99.35/t in June vs €83.65 a year ago, signalling much better crush margins. That's pulling seed basis tighter to Euronext (dap Constanta just €6.84/t under futures in June vs €20.29 last year). Constructive: margin-led strength expressed in the forward curve, not spot.

Government is urged to reconsider a ban on rapeseed imports from Ukraine, which would help cover part of the shortfall: Mariusz Szeliga, president of the Polish Association of Oil Producers, or PSPO

This year’s rapeseed harvest is seen 2.8m–3.2m tons, down from 3.6m tons in 2025, they say citing Poland’s statistics office

Rapeseed area was cut by about 9% to 1m ha due to difficult growing conditions, including winter loss and spring drought: Juliusz Młodecki, president of the National Association of Rapeseed and Protein Crop Producers, or KZPRiRB

Poland is EU’s 3rd largest rapeseed producer but it’s likely to be overtaken by Romania this year

Biodiesel/EU policy: Parliament rejected the soy high-ILUC classification 388–248 — a near-zero-odds outcome per industry — forcing the Commission to redraft with post-2008 data and double-cropping accounted. Keeps SBO in the RED-eligible crop pool and dilutes structural support for RSO, though France, Netherlands, Denmark and Belgium are already phasing out soy nationally. Malaysia moved to B15 and FGV expanded its B100 trial.

China/Palm: China's US soybean buying is confirmed and growing — ~330,000t by SOEs on 6 July plus 472,000t reported by USDA — but private crushers wait on tariff removal with USG offers 47¢ over Brazil; CBOT reaction was muted. China palm olein stocks rose above 740,000t (+40% y/y), pushing spot cfr south China down $15 to $1,145/t and capping the Asian spot complex short-term.
