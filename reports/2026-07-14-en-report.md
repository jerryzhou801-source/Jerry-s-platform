Rapeseed/RSO/Logistics: The curve told the story — fob Dutch mill prompt +€5.50 to €1,246/t and ASO +€8.50 to €1,191 against NDJ -€2.50 to €1,173.50 and FMA -€0.50 to €1,162.50, with the near legs bid on stronger energy and expected Rhine disruption while deferreds tracked weaker seed. Rhine barges that normally carry 1,000t can load only 500t, and this week's western European heat could tighten restrictions further; the impact is muted for now because many crushers are in annual maintenance, but disruption could bite from next week — precisely when Germany's rapeseed harvest begins. German farmers are focused on executing earlier forward contracts with little fresh spot selling, extending the withholding pattern. Bunge declared force majeure on RSM sales from Mannheim after a technical outage (normal operations expected by week-end), adding a squeeze to a meal market already supported by low water and firmer demand — the meal-side strength that has been fattening crush margins all week now has an outage premium in it. Upstream, Ukrainian seed jumped again: cpt POC +$9.50 to $595/t mid, dap Constanta +€7 to €525.50/t, with the geopolitical bid running through the whole Black Sea complex. Net tone: near-curve bullish on physical friction — Rhine restrictions, Mannheim outage, German harvest-delivery risk — while the deferred curve says the market still expects normalisation; the prompt–NDJ kink is the tradeable expression.

Black Sea risk/SFO: Argus quantified the war premium in Ukrainian wheat structures and the logic reads across to the oilseed complex: the fob–cpt POC spread has widened to about $14.50/t versus a $10 handling norm, meaning a \~$4.50/t security premium is being pushed down onto inland suppliers, as insurers re-model after the fatal Togo-flagged vessel strike and the suspension of a 25,000 t/d, 10mn t/yr Chornomorsk grain terminal bottlenecks the Odesa cluster. Producers are limiting sales to immediate liquidity needs — thin spot trade despite record crops. SFO is the vegoil transmission channel: destination offers rose to $1,440/t cif Mersin (from $1,410 a week ago) and $1,460–1,465 cif India (versus \~$1,430 trades last week), driven by the Kerch closure — which strands Russian SFO exports from Torgovyy and Yeysk, a quarter of Turkey's receipts this year (116,000t) — plus Kernel's suspended Chornomorsk terminal and spilled oil. NWE six-ports SFO held flat at $1,462.50, so the risk premium is so far a destination/cif phenomenon rather than a fob NWE one. Net tone: the Black Sea premium is real, quantified, and being paid by inland sellers and cif buyers first; watch whether it migrates into NWE fob quotes if disruptions persist.

Biofuels/US demand/China: The demand backdrop keeps thickening. US EIA data show biofuel producers consumed over 1.22bn lb of SBO in April (+48% y/y) and 365mn lb of canola oil (+170% y/y), with USDA's latest WASDE putting 2026-27 biofuel SBO use at 8.07mn t (+23% y/y) under the record federal mandate — and the crush build-out to match (AGP's first-ever all-SBO unit train, Cargill Regina online, LDC Yorkton expanding to 240,000 bu/d). Brazil stacked policy on top: CNPE raised the anhydrous ethanol blend to 32% from 30% for 180 days on Middle East energy-security grounds, and Bunge signed its largest South American SBO deal — 300,000 t/yr for five years to Acelen's $3bn+ Bahia biorefinery (17,340 b/d SAF/HVO by 2029). In China, ZCE RSO shrugged off the Australian import approval — the September contract settled at 9,910 yuan/t, up on the week — because imported canola economics remain thin, Australian new-crop was revised lower with arrivals only late-year, and Canadian weather risk keeps the underlying tightness intact; Argus flags improved Canadian weather as the main downside trigger. June soybean imports hit a record 13.55mn t (H1 record 50.39mn t), with record arrivals and 7% w/w SBM inventory builds capping China's meal market even as crushers run hard. Net tone: structural vegoil demand (US mandate, Brazil SAF/ethanol) keeps tightening the global oils balance beneath the geopolitical noise; ZCE RSO's resilience validates the tight-China-rapeseed thesis.
